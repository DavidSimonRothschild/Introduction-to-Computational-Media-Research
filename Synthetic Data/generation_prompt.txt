PROMPT FOR GENERATING SWISS GERMAN SYNTHETIC SOCIAL MEDIA STATEMENTS
=====================================================================

TASK OVERVIEW:
Create 100 synthetic social media statements written in authentic Swiss German dialect that represent different generational perspectives on various topics.

DISTRIBUTION REQUIREMENTS:
--------------------------

Topic Distribution:
- Politics, elections, and voting: 80% (80 statements)
- Travel and food: 20% (20 statements)

Generation Distribution:
- Millennials/Gen Y (1981-1996): 35% (35 statements)
- Gen Z (1997-2012): 40% (40 statements)
- Gen Alpha (2013+): 5% (5 statements)
- Gen X (1965-1980): 10% (10 statements)
- Baby Boomers (1946-1964): 10% (10 statements)

CONTENT REQUIREMENTS:
---------------------

Topics:
- Politics: voting/elections/referendums, party politics, immigration, climate policy, healthcare, taxes, economy, EU relations, neutrality debates, transportation, housing policy, language/cultural issues
- Travel/Food: Swiss cuisine, restaurants, travel experiences, public transport, weekend trips, food prices, regional specialties, vacation planning

Generational Perspectives:
- Millennials: Work-life balance, social justice, climate awareness, housing affordability, digital natives
- Gen Z: Climate crisis urgency, mental health, diversity, direct communication, highly digital
- Gen Alpha: Fully digital natives, short attention spans, very casual language, gaming/streaming culture
- Gen X: Pragmatic, independent, skeptical of institutions, direct and cynical
- Baby Boomers: Traditional values, more formal language, reference past experiences

LANGUAGE AND DIALECT REQUIREMENTS:
-----------------------------------

Write in authentic Swiss German dialects from German-speaking Switzerland ONLY. Do NOT include:
- Southern German dialects
- Alsatian dialects
- Standard German

Use authentic Swiss German features:
- "isch" instead of "ist"
- "chönd/chöi" instead of "können"
- "gah/gaa/goh" instead of "gehen"
- "het/hät" instead of "hat"
- "ned/nid/nöd/nit" instead of "nicht"
- "chli/bitzeli" instead of "klein/bisschen"
- Regional vocabulary from Zurich, Bern, Basel, St. Gallen, Central Swiss regions
- No preterite tense (use perfect tense)
- Swiss diminutives with "-li"

STYLE GUIDELINES:
-----------------

- Conversational and authentic to social media
- Vary sentence length and complexity naturally
- Include hashtags, emojis, or social media conventions appropriate to each generation
- Match communication style to generational patterns
- Gen Z/Alpha: Heavy emoji use, casual language, slang
- Millennials: Moderate emoji use, mix of formal and casual
- Gen X: Less emoji use, direct and skeptical
- Baby Boomers: Minimal/no emojis, more formal, complete sentences

OUTPUT FORMAT:
--------------

Numbered statements from 1 to 100, each on its own line:

1. [First statement in Swiss German]
2. [Second statement in Swiss German]
...
100. [Hundredth statement in Swiss German]
